FAQ

How does West March Work?

Each session will be scheduled by a player. The game is an "open-world sandbox" game. This means that the overall story of the "campaign" will be loose. There might be an overall story to each session; however, that story will be given through more subtle means. The Sandbox aspect means that the story is formed through the character's exploration of the world.

How are parties formed?

Parties are formed when a session is created. If a player sees something interesting that they want to explore, then they can create a session on Discord and claim what they want to investigate in the world. This can be anything. If your character hears a rumor about a cave where the acid pits change copper into gold, then your character can decide to explore that. The "Session Declaration" on Discord will be telling the DMs of the server that the "Alchemy Acid Pits" is something that you are interested in. It is then up to the DM to create that area and make it interesting for the session.

A DM can then claim the session and begin preparing for it. ALSO, players who want to join you in exploration, can sign-up for the session. The time and date of the session are chosen by the person creating the session. The player who created the session can have rules about how many players are able to join, and/or a specific DM they want to run the session. When the time comes, if there isn't enough interest (usually evident the day before), then the player can reschedule.

How Do I start Playing?

Look at the surrounding areas that were given in the world. If there are rumors you would like to explore, start there! If there is only a map, talk with a DM you want to play with, perhaps they can create something for you/your group.
